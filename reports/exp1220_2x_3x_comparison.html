⚠️ Risk Reality Check

🔴 2022 is the Danger Year at Both Levels

2022 was a prolonged bear market with high VIX. Credit spreads still profited massively (the strategy works in ALL regimes), but drawdowns were the worst of any year.

  • At 2x: 12.8% drawdown — just barely over the 12% North Star limit
  • At 3x: 18.8% drawdown — nearly 1 in 5 dollars at risk at the peak
  • Backtest DD is typically 60-70% of real-world DD (slippage, timing, gaps)
  • Realistic 2x DD estimate: ~15-18% in a bad year
  • Realistic 3x DD estimate: ~22-28% in a bad year
💡 Why Credit Spreads Handle Leverage Better Than Most Strategies
  • Defined risk: Max loss per trade is fixed (spread width). No tail risk blowups.
  • No margin call risk: Credit spreads use collateral, not margin borrowing. At 2-3x you're running 2-3 overlapping positions, not borrowing on margin.
  • 91% win rate: Losses are rare, and when they happen, they're capped at spread width.
  • Leverage here = more concurrent positions, not margin debt. Fundamentally safer than leveraged equity.
