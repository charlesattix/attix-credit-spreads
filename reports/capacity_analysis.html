Key Finding: Bimodal Capacity

SPY-only strategies (EXP-400, 503, 1220) scale to $1B+ because SPY options trade 3.1M contracts/day with $0.03 bid-ask spreads. ETF pair strategies (GLD-TLT, XLI-TLT) are limited to $20-200M by the much thinner option markets of GLD (43K/day), TLT (60K/day), and XLI (38K/day). The portfolio-level bottleneck is whichever ETF pair strategy receives the largest allocation.

7. North Star: Path to $1B+ AUM

Can we reach $1B?

YES, but only through SPY-dominant allocation.

  • $0-10M: All strategies viable. Pairs provide maximum diversification. Combined Sharpe highest here.
  • $10-100M: Cap pair strategies at $5-20M each. Route remaining capital to SPY strategies (EXP-400/503/1220).
  • $100M-1B: SPY strategies only. EXP-1220 (tail risk) + EXP-400/503 (credit spreads). Use VWAP execution, split across multiple expiry cycles.
  • $1B+: Requires market-making approach. Split across 5+ expiry cycles, 3-5 strike widths, intraday execution windows. Estimated max: $2-5B before SPY options impact becomes material.

Critical constraints:

  • GLD options: 43K/day total → hard cap ~$50M allocated to GLD pairs
  • TLT options: 60K/day total → hard cap ~$80M allocated to TLT strategies
  • XLI options: 38K/day total → hard cap ~$40M allocated to XLI pairs
  • SPY options: 3.1M/day total → can absorb $5B+ with smart execution
  • IBIT options: ~5K/day → hard cap ~$5M
