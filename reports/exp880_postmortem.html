Finding 1: The ML Model Was Never In The Backtester

CRITICAL: Zero ML filtering in backtest engine

Grep for ml_enhanced, ensemble_threshold, signal_model, use_ensemble, confidence_sizing in backtest/backtester.py returns zero matches.

The "ML Production Ensemble" with P≥0.75 threshold, XGBoost+RF+ExtraTrees, confidence-graded sizing — none of it was ever integrated into the Backtester class. It exists only in compass/production_ensemble.py for the live trading pipeline.

Both the original heuristic backtest (76.9% CAGR) and this IronVault backtest ran with zero ML filtering. The entire ML layer was theater for the backtest.

Lesson: Always verify that claimed features are actually wired into the execution path being tested.

Finding 2: Catastrophic Win/Loss Asymmetry

CRITICAL: Avg win $1,149 vs avg loss $4,213 (3.7x ratio)

Credit spreads have a structural asymmetry: small credits collected vs large potential losses. With $12-wide spreads and ~$0.60 credits (2% OTM), the max gain is ~$60/contract but max loss is ~$1,140/contract. The profit target at 55% captures even less.

At 8.5% flat risk per trade on $100K, each position risks ~$8,500. A stopped-out position loses the full $8,500. A profitable position captures ~$1,100 (credit × 55% × contracts).

You need 88% win rate to break even at this asymmetry. Actual: 71.4%.

Lesson: Credit spread strategies need either (a) much higher win rates, (b) much smaller position sizes, or (c) tighter stop losses to survive the inherent asymmetry.

The combo regime detector uses lagging indicators (MA200, RSI, VIX structure). During V-shaped recoveries (COVID April-May 2020, post-election Nov 2020, Aug 2024), the regime stays "bear" while the market reverses sharply upward — bear call spreads get destroyed.

Lesson: Lagging regime detectors + directional spreads = catching knives during transitions. Consider: (a) puts-only, (b) leading indicators for regime changes, (c) VIX-gated bear call sizing.

Lessons Learned

1. ML model was never in the backtest loop. The entire ML layer (ensemble, confidence sizing, disagreement scaling) existed only in the live pipeline. Backtest results were pure regime+technical, inflated by heuristic pricing.
2. Heuristic pricing hid the win/loss asymmetry. With synthetic credit fractions, spreads appeared to collect $1.50-2.50 credit. Real IronVault data shows credits of $0.40-0.80 for 2% OTM 12-wide puts. The math doesn't work at those levels.
3. Bear calls during regime transitions are a loaded gun. The combo regime detector lags by design (MA200-based). V-shaped recoveries destroy bear call positions before the regime detector catches up.
4. 8.5% flat risk per trade is too aggressive. Credit spread strategies need position sizing proportional to the credit received, not a fixed percentage of capital. A $0.60 credit on a $12 spread is 5% credit-to-width — far too thin for 8.5% risk.
5. Crisis Hedge V2 was never integrated into the Backtester. The dynamic delevering that supposedly protected against drawdowns was a separate module (compass/crisis_hedge_v2.py) that the Backtester class never calls. All reported "crisis-hedged" backtest results were fiction.
