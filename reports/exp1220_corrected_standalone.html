EXP-1220 Corrected Standalone Analysis

Real IronVault trades | 3-way comparison: buggy vs corrected vs with-hedge

BUG DIAGNOSIS — Why SPY-only portfolio showed -0.7% CAGR:

Bug 1 (dilution): Daily returns = trade P&L / $100K on exit dates, 0.0 on other days. With ~171 trade exits over ~1475 trading days, that's 65% exposure. The other ~35% of days contribute zero to mean but inflate denominator → Sharpe crushed.

Bug 2 (over-hedging): Hedge cost $17.30/day charged on ALL 1475 trading days, but trades only have capital at risk for ~957 hold-days total. Overpaying by 1475/957 = 1.5x.

Bug 3 (leverage on zeros): 1.6x leverage applied to the diluted series amplifies the negative hedge drag on zero-return days.
FIX: Compute metrics at the trade level (cumulative P&L, not daily returns). Hedge cost charged per hold-day (days with open position), not all calendar days. This matches how the EXP-1220 backtest originally computed its 77% CAGR.
