1. Strategy Overview

Thesis

The US Treasury yield curve is historically mean-reverting. Sustained steepness flattens via central bank action; inversions resolve via cuts. The log(TLT/SHY) ratio captures curve shape in ETF terms: high = long end overpriced (bet on flattening), low = front end overpriced (bet on steepening).

Signal

  • Compute log(TLT/SHY) daily, rolling 252-day z-score
  • Entry: |z| > 1.5 (enter curve mean-reversion trade)
  • Direction: z > 1.5 = flatten (short TLT / long SHY). z < -1.5 = steepen (long TLT / short SHY)
  • Exit: |z| < 0.5 (revert) OR 60 days max hold

Risk model

2% account risk per trade, split equally between the two legs. Real execution costs: $0.005/share commission + 2 bps round-trip slippage per leg.
