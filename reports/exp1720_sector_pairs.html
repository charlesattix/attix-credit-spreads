Method

  • Cointegration: Engle-Granger residual stationarity test (statsmodels.tsa.stattools.coint) AND Johansen trace test (statsmodels.tsa.vector_ar.vecm.coint_johansen) at rank 0. Both must reject the null at 5% to qualify.
  • Hedge ratio: OLS β on log prices. Static (full-sample) for the headline backtest; re-estimated each fold for walk-forward.
  • Z-score: 60-day rolling, computed on the spread, shifted by 1 day so today's signal uses yesterday's stats (no look-ahead).
  • Trading rules: enter at |z|>2, exit at |z|<0.5, hard stop at |z|>4. 5 bps cost on every position flip.
  • Walk-forward: expanding window (2y train minimum, 1y test slices). Hedge ratio re-estimated each fold.

Honesty

  • Static hedge ratio in headline numbers — the portfolio CAGR/Sharpe above use the full-sample OLS β, which is mildly look-ahead biased. WF folds re-estimate β each window — those numbers are the OOS truth.
  • Cointegration is not stable across regimes. Pairs that test cointegrated 2015-2025 may not be cointegrated in any single sub-window. This is a known issue with sector pairs (sector composition shifts, mega-cap concentration changes the effective hedge).
  • 5 bps round-trip is optimistic for retail but achievable on liquid sector ETFs at institutional rates.
  • EXP-1220 reference is the proxy, not the validated real trades — directional correlation is the meaningful signal here.
