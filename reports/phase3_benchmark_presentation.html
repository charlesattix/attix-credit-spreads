⚠️ Verdict: ML can't improve CS trade selection

Why? When 84 out of 100 trades already win, the model learns "just say yes to everything." Both models give almost every trade a probability above 50%, so they never skip a trade. The only bad year (2022, 60% WR) is a regime problem — the whole market was bearish — not a trade-selection problem.

What ML CAN do for CS: (1) Detect when to pause trading entirely (regime gate), (2) Scale position sizes up/down based on confidence, (3) NOT pick individual trades to skip.

In bull markets, both models perform similarly — the market does the heavy lifting. But in bear markets and high-vol periods (exactly when you need ML most), the Ensemble is significantly better. It's like having a cool-headed pilot vs one who panics in turbulence.

4 What This Means — Strategic Recommendations

✓
Use the Ensemble model, not XGBoost alone
It wins on AUC in every fold and stays robust in bear/high-vol markets. The cost? It takes 6 seconds vs 0.2 seconds to train. Worth it.
!
Don't use ML to pick individual credit spread trades
With an 84% base win rate, both models just say "yes" to everything. Neither filters a meaningful number of trades. The edge is already baked into the strategy rules.
→
Use ML as a regime gate + position sizer for CS
Instead of skip/take decisions, use the Ensemble to: (1) detect bear regimes and pause CS trading, (2) scale position sizes from 3→7 contracts based on model confidence. This is where the real money is.
✓
For mixed strategies (CS + straddles + IC), ML filtering works
When you include riskier strategy types, the Ensemble lifts the ML-filtered win rate to 83.7% from a 59.5% base — a +24 percentage point improvement.
★
Bear & high-vol regimes = where ML earns its keep
In calm bull markets, ML adds marginal value. In turbulent conditions, the Ensemble is the difference between profit and disaster. Build the system to lean on ML more when things get rough.
