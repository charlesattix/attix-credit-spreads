3. Critical Findings

3 Strategies Pass Full Audit (Grade A)

EXP-1630 GLD/TLT (explicit 2yr IS / 3yr OOS, hardcoded params), Vol Term Structure (4 expanding windows, OOS = full-period Sharpe), Cross-Asset Pairs (5 rolling windows, 100% positive). These are the only strategies with rigorous, documented walk-forward validation.

6 of 14 Experiments Have NO Walk-Forward Validation

EXP-880, EXP-1220, EXP-1230, EXP-1270, EXP-1320, EXP-1470 — all parameters were optimized on the full dataset with no IS/OOS separation. Any reported Sharpe includes look-ahead bias. The synthetic-to-real collapse (EXP-880: -104%, EXP-1470: 0.42% CAGR) is direct evidence of this overfitting.

Selection Bias in XLI Iron Condors

34 configurations tested on the same 40 trades, best selected post-hoc. OOS Sharpe (8.58) exceeding IS (3.89) is a classic signature of optimization bias, not genuine alpha. The "OOS" period was contaminated by config selection.

Sparse OOS Samples

5 experiments have <20 OOS trades. EXP-1640 has only 6 OOS trades (Sharpe -0.12). EXP-1470 has only 19 total trades. Statistical power for validation requires ≥30 OOS trades (preferably ≥5 per tunable parameter).

Transaction Cost Gaps

EXP-1220 (tail risk) does not model real SPY put/VIX call transaction costs — it uses a flat 2% annual hedge budget assumption. Real costs depend on VIX level, term structure, and strike selection. Cross-Asset Pairs use equity prices without option execution costs.

4. Required Standards for Future Experiments

Minimum Requirements for "Deployable" Status

  1. Explicit IS/OOS split — minimum 2:1 ratio (2yr IS, 1yr OOS)
  2. Parameter:trade ratio — maximum 0.20 (5+ OOS trades per tunable parameter)
  3. OOS Sharpe stability — within 50% of IS Sharpe (flag if OOS > IS)
  4. Real transaction costs — IronVault option prices, not synthetic
  5. Regime validation — test across bull/bear/high_vol independently
  6. No post-hoc selection — single configuration, not best-of-N
  7. Minimum 30 OOS trades for statistical significance
