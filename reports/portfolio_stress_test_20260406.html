6. HONESTY — what this report does NOT say

  • EXP-1220 is a proxy, not real trades. The live-tradeable EXP-1220 (compass/exp1220_standalone.py) was validated separately at 77% CAGR / Sharpe 5.78 / 11% DD on real IronVault options. The proxy here uses a dynamic-leverage theta model on real Yahoo SPY that yields 87% CAGR / Sharpe 7.9 / DD 8.1% — it slightly understates downside variance. Treat the combined portfolio numbers as upper bound on Sharpe and lower bound on max DD.
  • EXP-1660 daily series is sparse. Only 34 trades in the hardened set — PnL is concentrated on a handful of exit dates. Block bootstrap of such a series can re-sample the same positive trade multiple times, biasing MC paths toward optimism.
  • EXP-1820 trades cluster in time. 89 trades over ~5.5 years means most days are zero. Real-time deployment will have execution slippage, IV surface errors, and sector-spread availability constraints not captured here.
  • Block bootstrap assumes regime stationarity. If the joint return distribution shifts (regime change, vol-of-vol spike, new market structure), the MC envelope will systematically understate tail risk. The 2020 COVID period is included in the bootstrap pool, but a worse 1-day shock than what's in the sample cannot be generated by resampling.
  • Correlations are not stable across regimes. Even when the full-sample average is low, individual pair correlations spike during specific crises (see correlation tables — the 2022 bear market shows different structure than COVID).
  • No execution costs at the portfolio level. Each strategy's costs are baked into its own series, but rebalancing the portfolio weights, FX, borrow on shorts (EXP-1780 long-short), and options assignment are not modeled.
  • Survivorship bias. All four strategies are the winning configs from a much larger search. The OOS performance of any individual one is lower than the IS numbers shown.

Summary

Under the assumptions above, an equal-ish weight portfolio of the four strategies shows median 1y return +32%, 5th-percentile return +21%, and P95 max drawdown 4.6%. Crisis replay shows the portfolio lost money in 4/6 historical crisis periods. Correlations broke through crises (delta -0.374).
