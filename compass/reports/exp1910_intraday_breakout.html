Methodology & honest caveats

  • Signal: at the open, take a long if (open−prev_close)/prev_close ≥ +g, short if ≤ −g, AND today's volume ≥ k × 20-day average. Exit at today's close.
  • P&L: literal (close−open)/open from real Yahoo OHLC, minus a 1-bp round-trip cost on every entered day. No interpolation, no synthetic fills.
  • Walk-forward: 252d train / 63d OOS / 63d step. Per fold, grid-search g∈[0.001, 0.002, 0.003, 0.005] × k∈[1.0, 1.25, 1.5, 2.0] by training Sharpe; apply best OOS only.
  • What this is NOT: a true intraday backtest. Daily OHLC cannot model intraday stops, scaling, or minute-bar momentum. Anything that requires intra-bar execution (trailing stop, VWAP entry, scale-in) needs minute data which Yahoo does not provide. The Sharpe ceiling of a daily-resolution proxy is therefore much lower than the Sharpe of the 'real' minute-bar version of the same idea.
  • On the Sharpe-5+ target: a Sharpe-5+ daily series from 1 trade-day≤signal frequency on a single underlying is extraordinary and should be treated as overfit until proven OOS for ≥6 months on live execution. The walk-forward design above gives one honest read on whether the edge survives at all.
  • Cost assumption: 1bp round-trip is generous on SPY (half-spread ~0.5bp). Tightening to a more realistic 2bp shifts metrics modestly; the file's ROUND_TRIP_BPS knob lets you re-run.
