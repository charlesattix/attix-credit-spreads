Research & planning · North Star v8a · 2026-04-08 · The only unmet North Star target
Executive summary. The current $50M soft cap is gated by SLV calendar options liquidity (SI=F futures $122M/day ADV). Four weight-reallocation attempts (EXP-2350 / 2380 / 2430 / 2480) all failed — this is not a weight problem, it's a data-coverage problem. Scaling past $50M requires adding new uncorrelated underlyings, and adding underlyings requires data subscriptions beyond IronVault's current universe. The path is $50M → $100M → $500M → $1B across four investable tiers with total infrastructure cost of $2.4K/month operational and one-time onboarding costs of ~$15K.

The 8-stream North Star v8a portfolio binds at $50M soft cap because four of the eight sleeves have ADV (Average Daily Volume) in the $100-200M range at our production weights. The binding sleeve rotates as weights change, but every weight schedule we've tried hits a ceiling somewhere around $50M.

Three real data gaps, not just low ADV:
  1. SLV options: IronVault has zero SLV contracts. We use SI=F futures as a proxy, but SI=F front-month is only $122M/day — this is the hardest bottleneck.
  2. IWM options: Zero in IronVault. Cross-vol arbitrage currently runs on ETF share prices only, which underestimates the real capacity of the variance arbitrage strategy.
  3. VIX calls (CBOE VX): Zero in IronVault. Crisis Alpha v5 uses UVXY + VXX as a VIX-call proxy, which caps the sleeve at ~$72M soft.

Key insight: infrastructure cost is a rounding error at every stage. The real cost of scaling is market impact (EXP-2140 square-root model), which the roadmap addresses by adding new uncorrelated underlyings. Each new sleeve lifts the portfolio capacity floor without materially degrading Sharpe, because the new streams are genuinely orthogonal (ρ < 0.3 to EXP-1220, per the EXP-2220 eigenvalue decomposition showing 6.69/7 effective independent streams in the current 7-stream portfolio).

8. Go / No-Go Decision Path

Recommendation: begin the S1 data subscriptions (Polygon Options Starter, $199/mo) in parallel with Phase 9 paper trading. The $200/mo spend is negligible versus the option value of unlocking IWM, SLV, and VIX-call backfill BEFORE we need them at the T5 ($50M) live tranche. This avoids a capacity wall 6 months from now.
  1. Now (pre-Phase 9): subscribe to Polygon Options Starter, backfill IWM + SLV + VIX history into IronVault.
  2. Phase 9 paper + T1 live ($25K): validate the 8-stream v8a portfolio as-is. No new sleeves.
  3. T2 live ($100K) + S1 unlock: add IWM real CS to vol_arb, drop SLV calendar to 2%, add EXP-2580 SPY-weekly. Expected capacity lift to $100M soft cap.
  4. T3 live ($1M): subscribe to CBOE DataShop, backfill VIX calls, replace UVXY proxy in Crisis Alpha v5.
  5. T4 live ($10M) + S2 unlock: add XLE, XLK sector sleeves. Portfolio becomes 10 streams. File Form ADV if AUM > $110M.
  6. T5 live ($50M): begin S3 work (prime broker onboarding, NY4 colocation, quant hire). Budget becomes $23K/mo.
  7. T6 live ($100M): migrate to IBKR Pro / Clear Street.
  8. T7 live ($500M) + S3 complete: add SPX + NDX direct index sleeves. 12-14 stream portfolio.
  9. T8 live ($1B+): S4 operational scale — SOC 2, DR, second hire, single-name sleeves.
